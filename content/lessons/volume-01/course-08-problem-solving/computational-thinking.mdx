## Computational Thinking

**Computational thinking** is a problem-solving method that uses concepts from computer science.

### The Four Pillars

| Pillar | Description | Example |
|--------|-------------|---------|
| **Decomposition** | Breaking a problem into smaller parts | Planning a trip: flights, hotel, activities |
| **Pattern Recognition** | Finding similarities | Noticing all TODO apps need CRUD operations |
| **Abstraction** | Focusing on important details only | A map shows roads, not every tree |
| **Algorithm Design** | Creating step-by-step solutions | A recipe for baking a cake |

### Why It Matters

Computational thinking helps you solve problems in any field — not just programming. It's about thinking clearly and systematically.
