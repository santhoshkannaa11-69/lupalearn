## 1. Hook

Every digital photo, song, video, and document on your computer is ultimately stored as a sequence of bits — millions of 0s and 1s. The bit is the atom of digital information.

## 2. Core Concept

A **bit** (binary digit) is the smallest unit of data in computing.

| Bit Value | Physical Representation |
|-----------|------------------------|
| **0** | No voltage, switch off, no charge |
| **1** | Voltage present, switch on, charged |

### How Many Values?

| Bits | Possible Values | Example |
|------|-----------------|---------|
| 1 | 2 | 0, 1 |
| 2 | 4 | 00, 01, 10, 11 |
| 3 | 8 | 000 → 111 |
| 8 (1 byte) | 256 | 00000000 → 11111111 |
| n | 2ⁿ | Exponential growth |

## 3. Real-World Scale

| Storage | Bits | Bytes |
|---------|------|-------|
| **1 KB** | 8,192 | 1,024 |
| **1 MB** | 8,388,608 | 1,048,576 |
| **1 GB** | 8,589,934,592 | 1,073,741,824 |

## 4. Summary

- A **bit** is the smallest unit of data — 0 or 1
- Bits are physically represented as **voltage states** in circuits
- More bits = exponentially more possible values
