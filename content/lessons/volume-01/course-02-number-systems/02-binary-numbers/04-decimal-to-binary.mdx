## 1. Hook

Converting decimal to binary is like making change with the fewest coins — you always take the largest denomination that fits.

## 2. Method 1: Division by 2

The most reliable method. Repeatedly divide by 2, reading remainders backward.

### Example: Convert 42 to Binary

```
42 ÷ 2 = 21 remainder 0  (LSB)
21 ÷ 2 = 10 remainder 1
10 ÷ 2 = 5  remainder 0
 5 ÷ 2 = 2  remainder 1
 2 ÷ 2 = 1  remainder 0
 1 ÷ 2 = 0  remainder 1  (MSB)

Reading remainders bottom to top: 101010
```

## 3. Method 2: Largest Power

Find the largest power of 2 that fits, subtract it, repeat.

### Example: Convert 42 to Binary

```
42 - 32 = 10  → 1 × 32  (position 5)
10 - 8  = 2   → 1 × 8   (position 3)
 2 - 2  = 0   → 1 × 2   (position 1)
               → 0 × 16, 0 × 4, 0 × 1

Result: 101010
```

## 5. Common Mistakes

| Mistake | Why | Fix |
|---------|-----|-----|
| Reading remainders top-to-bottom | First remainder is LSB, not MSB | Read bottom to top |
| Forgetting to divide until 0 | Stops too early | Continue until quotient is 0 |

## 6. Summary

- **Division by 2**: divide repeatedly, read remainders backward
- **Largest power**: subtract the biggest power of 2 that fits
- Both methods give the same result — use whichever you prefer
