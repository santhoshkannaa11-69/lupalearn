## 1. Hook

Every photo on your phone is stored as a grid of colored dots (pixels). Every song is stored as a series of snapshots (samples) of sound waves. Every video is a sequence of images played fast enough to create motion.

## 2. Images

An image is a **grid of pixels**. Each pixel has a color value.

| Format | Bits Per Pixel | Colors |
|--------|---------------|--------|
| **Grayscale** | 8 | 256 shades |
| **RGB (True Color)** | 24 | 16.7 million |
| **RGBA** | 32 | 16.7M + transparency |

### How a 3×3 Image Looks in Memory

```
Pixel data: [RGB, RGB, RGB, RGB, RGB, RGB, RGB, RGB, RGB]
Each pixel: [R=8 bits][G=8 bits][B=8 bits]
Total: 9 × 24 = 216 bits = 27 bytes
```

## 3. Audio

Audio is stored as **samples** of sound waves:

| Quality | Sample Rate | Bit Depth | Size (per minute) |
|---------|-------------|-----------|-------------------|
| **Phone** | 8,000 Hz | 8-bit | ~5 MB |
| **CD** | 44,100 Hz | 16-bit | ~10 MB |
| **Studio** | 96,000 Hz | 24-bit | ~35 MB |

### The Nyquist Theorem

You must sample at **at least 2× the highest frequency** to accurately reproduce sound. Human hearing goes up to ~20 kHz, so CDs use 44.1 kHz.

## 4. Video

Video = **images × time**.

A 1080p video at 30 fps needs:
```
1,920 × 1,080 = 2,073,600 pixels per frame
2,073,600 × 24 bits = ~6 MB per frame
6 MB × 30 fps = ~180 MB per second
```

Compression (H.264, VP9) reduces this by 100-1000×.

## 5. Summary

- **Images** = grids of colored pixels (RGB values)
- **Audio** = samples of sound waves (amplitude over time)
- **Video** = sequences of images (frames per second)
- **Compression** makes all of these practical for storage and streaming
