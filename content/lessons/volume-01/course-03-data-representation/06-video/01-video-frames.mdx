## 1. Hook

A video is a sequence of still images displayed fast enough to create the illusion of motion. At 24 frames per second, your brain blends the images into continuous movement.

## 2. Key Concepts

| Term | Definition | Examples |
|------|------------|----------|
| **Frame** | A single still image in the sequence | 1920 × 1080 pixels |
| **Frame rate (FPS)** | How many frames per second | 24 (film), 30 (TV), 60 (smooth) |
| **Keyframe** | A full frame (reference) | Every ~1-2 seconds |
| **Delta frame** | Only the changes from the last frame | 95% smaller than a keyframe |

### File Size (Uncompressed)

```
1080p × 24-bit × 30 fps = 6.22 MB × 30 = 186 MB/s
186 MB/s × 60 = ~11 GB/minute
```

## 3. Why Compression Is Essential

Without compression, a 2-hour movie at 1080p would be ~1.3 TB. With H.264 compression, it's ~4 GB. That's 300× smaller.

## 4. Summary

- Video = **sequence of frames** at a rate (fps)
- **Keyframes** are full images; **delta frames** store only changes
- Uncompressed video is **enormous** — compression is essential
