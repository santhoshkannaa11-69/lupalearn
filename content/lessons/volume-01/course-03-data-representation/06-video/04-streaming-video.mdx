## 1. Hook

When you watch a YouTube video, the first few seconds buffer while the video quality adjusts to your internet speed. This is **adaptive bitrate streaming** — the video player picks the best quality your connection can handle.

## 2. How Streaming Works

```
Video file (server)
    ↓
Split into small chunks (~2-10 seconds each)
    ↓
Encoded at multiple bitrates (360p, 720p, 1080p, 4K)
    ↓
Player requests chunks adaptively
    ↓
Player buffer (a few seconds ahead)
    ↓
Rendered on screen
```

## 3. Adaptive Bitrate

| Network Speed | Best Quality |
|--------------|--------------|
| **5 Mbps+** | 1080p |
| **25 Mbps+** | 4K |
| **Under 2 Mbps** | 480p |

The player constantly monitors your connection and switches quality seamlessly.

## 4. Summary

- Streaming splits video into **small chunks**
- **Adaptive bitrate** picks the right quality for your connection
- **Buffering** prevents interruptions when the network fluctuates
