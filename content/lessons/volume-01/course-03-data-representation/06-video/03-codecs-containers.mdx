## 1. Hook

A video file ending in .mp4 tells you the container, not the codec. An MP4 can contain H.264 video or H.265 video. The codec is what actually compresses the video; the container is just the wrapper.

## 2. Codecs vs Containers

| Layer | What It Does | Examples |
|-------|-------------|----------|
| **Codec** | Compresses/decompresses video | H.264, H.265, AV1, VP9 |
| **Container** | Wraps video + audio + metadata | MP4, MKV, WebM, AVI |

## 3. Video Codec Comparison

| Codec | Efficiency | Hardware Support | Licensing |
|-------|-----------|-----------------|-----------|
| **H.264 (AVC)** | Good | Universal | Patented |
| **H.265 (HEVC)** | Better (~50% smaller) | Modern devices | Patented, complex |
| **VP9** | Better | Most browsers | Royalty-free |
| **AV1** | Best (~30% better than H.265) | Newer devices | Royalty-free |
| **AVC2** | Emerging | Very new | Royalty-free |

## 4. Summary

- **Codec** = compression algorithm; **Container** = file format
- H.264 is the most widely compatible codec
- AV1 is the next generation — smaller files, royalty-free
