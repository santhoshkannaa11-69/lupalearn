## 1. Hook

CD-quality audio captures 44,100 snapshots of sound every second. Each snapshot uses 16 bits. That's 705,600 bits per second of audio — just to reproduce sounds you can hear.

## 2. Core Concepts

### Sample Rate

How many times per second the sound wave is measured.

| Quality | Sample Rate | Highest Frequency |
|---------|-------------|-------------------|
| **Phone** | 8,000 Hz | 4 kHz |
| **CD** | 44,100 Hz | 22 kHz |
| **Studio** | 96,000 Hz | 48 kHz |

### Bit Depth

How many bits per sample.

| Bit Depth | Dynamic Range | Levels |
|-----------|---------------|--------|
| **8-bit** | 48 dB | 256 |
| **16-bit** | 96 dB | 65,536 |
| **24-bit** | 144 dB | 16.7 million |

## 3. The Nyquist Theorem

To accurately capture a frequency, you must sample at **at least 2× that frequency**.

Human hearing: ~20 kHz → CD sample rate: 44.1 kHz

## 4. Summary

- **Sample rate**: how many measurements per second
- **Bit depth**: precision of each measurement
- **Nyquist theorem**: sample at 2× the highest frequency
- Higher rates = better quality = larger files
