## 1. Hook

Spotify streams about 40 MB per hour of music. Without compression, that same hour would be 600 MB. Compression makes streaming services possible.

## 2. Audio Compression Pipeline

```
1. Convert to frequency domain
   (FFT — Fast Fourier Transform)
2. Apply psychoacoustic model
   (remove frequencies humans don't hear well)
3. Quantize
   (reduce precision of remaining frequencies)
4. Huffman encode
   (compress the result)
```

## 3. Bitrate Comparison

| Bitrate | Quality | File Size (3 min) | Use Case |
|---------|---------|-------------------|----------|
| **320 kbps** | Excellent | ~7 MB | High-quality MP3 |
| **256 kbps** | Very good | ~5.5 MB | AAC (typical) |
| **192 kbps** | Good | ~4 MB | Streaming standard |
| **128 kbps** | Fair | ~3 MB | Podcasts, speech |

## 4. Summary

- Audio compression uses **psychoacoustic models** to discard inaudible data
- Lower **bitrate** = smaller files = lower quality
- Streaming services use adaptive bitrate for varying network conditions
