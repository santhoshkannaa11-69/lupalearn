## 1. Hook

Sound is a wave moving through air. When you speak, your vocal cords vibrate the air, creating waves that travel to a listener's ear. A microphone captures these waves and converts them to an electrical signal — which the computer then samples into numbers.

## 2. Sound Wave Properties

| Property | What It Determines | Measured In |
|----------|-------------------|-------------|
| **Frequency** | Pitch (high/low) | Hertz (Hz) |
| **Amplitude** | Volume (loud/quiet) | Decibels (dB) |
| **Wavelength** | Distance between peaks | Meters |

## 3. The Digital Conversion

```
Analog sound wave (continuous)
    ↓
Microphone converts to electrical signal
    ↓
ADC (Analog to Digital Converter) samples at regular intervals
    ↓
Discrete digital values (numbers)
    ↓
Stored as binary
```

## 4. Summary

- Sound is a **wave** with frequency (pitch) and amplitude (volume)
- Computers convert analog waves to digital by **sampling**
- Higher frequencies require faster sampling
