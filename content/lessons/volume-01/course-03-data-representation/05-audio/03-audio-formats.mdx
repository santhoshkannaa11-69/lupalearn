## 1. Hook

A 3-minute song can be 30 MB (FLAC) or 3 MB (MP3). Same song. Same melody. Same lyrics. The difference is how much information was discarded.

## 2. Audio Format Comparison

| Format | Type | Compression | Size (per minute) |
|--------|------|-------------|-------------------|
| **WAV** | Uncompressed | None | ~10 MB |
| **FLAC** | Lossless | ~50% | ~5 MB |
| **ALAC** | Lossless | ~50% | ~5 MB |
| **MP3** | Lossy | ~90% | ~1 MB |
| **AAC** | Lossy | ~90% | ~1 MB |
| **OGG** | Lossy | ~90% | ~1 MB |

## 3. Perceptual Coding

MP3 uses **perceptual coding** — it discards frequencies the human ear can barely hear. This is why a 128 kbps MP3 sounds fine to most people, but an audiophile can hear the difference.

## 4. Summary

- **WAV/FLAC**: highest quality, largest files
- **MP3/AAC**: good quality, small files (perceptual coding)
- The best format depends on your ears and your storage
