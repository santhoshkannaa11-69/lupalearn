## 1. Hook

A kilobyte should be 1,000 bytes, right? Not exactly — to a computer, it's 1,024. That 24-byte difference adds up fast: a "1 TB" drive might actually have room for only 931 GB.

## 2. The Storage Hierarchy

| Unit | Bits | Bytes | Rough Equivalent |
|------|------|-------|------------------|
| **Bit** | 1 | — | A single 0 or 1 |
| **Nibble** | 4 | ½ | One hex digit |
| **Byte** | 8 | 1 | One character |
| **Kilobyte (KB)** | 8,192 | 1,024 | A paragraph |
| **Megabyte (MB)** | 8,388,608 | 1,048,576 | A photo |
| **Gigabyte (GB)** | ~8.6 billion | ~1 billion | A movie |
| **Terabyte (TB)** | ~8.8 trillion | ~1 trillion | A hard drive |

## 3. Binary vs Decimal Confusion

| Prefix | Binary (JEDEC) | Decimal (SI) |
|--------|----------------|--------------|
| **KB** | 1,024 | 1,000 |
| **MB** | 1,048,576 | 1,000,000 |
| **GB** | 1,073,741,824 | 1,000,000,000 |

This is why a "500 GB" hard drive shows as ~465 GB on your computer.

## 4. Summary

- **Bit** = smallest unit, **Byte** = 8 bits
- Storage units: KB, MB, GB, TB (each 1,024× the previous)
- Binary (1,024) vs decimal (1,000) causes the "missing space" problem
