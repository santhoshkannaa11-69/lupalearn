## 1. Hook

A 10-second video can be 2 MB or 200 MB. A photo can be crisp or pixelated. A song can be rich or tinny. The difference is always a choice about representation.

## 2. The Four Trade-Offs

| Factor | Question | Example |
|--------|----------|---------|
| **Accuracy** | How faithful is the representation? | Lossless vs lossy compression |
| **Storage** | How much space does it use? | RAW photo (25 MB) vs JPEG (2 MB) |
| **Performance** | How fast can it be processed? | Simple encoding = faster decoding |
| **Compatibility** | Can other systems read it? | PNG works everywhere, HEIC doesn't |

## 3. Real-World Examples

| Data Type | High Quality | Low Quality |
|-----------|-------------|-------------|
| **Audio** | FLAC (30 MB/song) | MP3 128kbps (4 MB/song) |
| **Image** | RAW (25 MB) | JPEG (2 MB) |
| **Video** | Blu-ray (50 GB) | YouTube 480p (500 MB) |

## 4. Summary

- Representation choices involve **trade-offs** between accuracy, storage, performance, and compatibility
- There's no single "best" format — it depends on the use case
- Understanding these trade-offs is essential for building real systems
