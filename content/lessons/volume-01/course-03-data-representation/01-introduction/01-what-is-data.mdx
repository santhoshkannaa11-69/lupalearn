## 1. Hook

A song on vinyl and a song on Spotify are the same song — but they're represented completely differently. One is a continuous groove in plastic. The other is a sequence of numbers. Both are "data," but understanding the difference between analog and digital representation is fundamental to computing.

## 2. Why This Matters

Every piece of information a computer processes — numbers, text, images, audio, video — must be represented as binary. Understanding *how* these representations work is the key to understanding how computers work.

## 3. Core Concept

**Data** is information stored in a form a computer can process.

| Aspect | Analog | Digital |
|--------|--------|---------|
| **Representation** | Continuous physical quantity | Discrete numbers |
| **Example** | Vinyl record groove | MP3 audio file |
| **Degradation** | Wears over time | Perfect copies |
| **Error tolerance** | Low | High (can be corrected) |

## 4. Mental Model — The Telephone Game

Analog is like whispering a message directly — it degrades as it travels. Digital is like writing the message down and handing it person to person — it stays the same.

## 5. Summary

- **Data** is information in a computer-processable form
- **Analog** = continuous, **digital** = discrete
- Digital data is stored as **binary** — sequences of 0s and 1s
