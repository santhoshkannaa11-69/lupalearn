## 1. Hook

Augustus De Morgan discovered two rules that are among the most useful in all of computing: NOT (A AND B) = NOT A OR NOT B. In other words, flipping AND to OR (or OR to AND) when you distribute a NOT.

## 2. De Morgan's Laws

| Law | Expression | Equivalent |
|-----|-----------|------------|
| **First** | ¬(A ∧ B) | ¬A ∨ ¬B |
| **Second** | ¬(A ∨ B) | ¬A ∧ ¬B |

### In Programming

```javascript
!(a && b) === !a || !b
!(a || b) === !a && !b
```

## 3. Truth Table Verification

```

```

## 5. Summary

- **De Morgan's Laws**: distribute NOT over AND/OR
- `!(A && B) = !A || !B`
- `!(A || B) = !A && !B`
- Essential for simplifying complex conditions in code
