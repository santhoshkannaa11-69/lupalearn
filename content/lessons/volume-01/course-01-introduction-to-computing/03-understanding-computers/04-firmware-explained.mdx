## 1. Hook

Every device with a computer chip has a tiny program permanently stored on it — invisible to the user, essential for operation. Your SSD has one. Your monitor has one. Your router has one. Even your keyboard has one.

## 2. What Is Firmware?

**Firmware** is software permanently stored on a hardware device. It's the middle layer between hardware and regular software.

| Layer | Modifiable By | Speed |
|-------|--------------|-------|
| **Software** | User (easy update) | Slowest |
| **Firmware** | Manufacturer (special process) | Fast |
| **Hardware** | Not modifiable | Fastest |

## 3. Common Firmware Examples

| Device | Firmware | What Happens Without It |
|--------|----------|------------------------|
| **Motherboard** | BIOS/UEFI | Computer can't boot |
| **SSD** | SSD firmware | Drive won't function |
| **Router** | Router firmware | No internet routing |
| **Printer** | Printer firmware | Can't process print jobs |

## 4. Summary

- Firmware is **semi-permanent software** on hardware devices
- It's **essential** for device operation
- Unlike regular software, updating firmware carries risk (a failed update can "brick" the device)
