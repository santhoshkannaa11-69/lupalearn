## Storage (HDD, SSD)

**Storage** is where data lives permanently — files, programs, your operating system.

### HDD (Hard Disk Drive)

Traditional magnetic storage.

- **How it works**: Spinning magnetic platters, a moving read/write head
- **Speed**: ~150 MB/s
- **Capacity**: Up to 20 TB
- **Durability**: Fragile (moving parts)
- **Noise**: Audible spinning and clicking
- **Cost**: ~$0.02/GB

### SSD (Solid State Drive)

Modern flash storage.

- **How it works**: NAND flash memory, no moving parts
- **Speed**: ~5,000 MB/s (NVMe)
- **Capacity**: Up to 4 TB (consumer)
- **Durability**: Very durable
- **Noise**: Silent
- **Cost**: ~$0.10/GB

### Comparison

| Factor | HDD | SSD |
|--------|-----|-----|
| Boot time | 30-60 seconds | 5-10 seconds |
| File transfer | 150 MB/s | 5,000 MB/s |
| Lifespan | 3-5 years | 5-10 years |
| Power use | 6-10 W | 2-4 W |
| Weight | Heavy | Light |

### Storage Hierarchy

```
Registers (1 ns)       ← Smallest, fastest
Cache (3 ns)           │
RAM (50 ns)            │
SSD (100,000 ns)       │
HDD (10,000,000 ns)    ← Largest, slowest
```

Every computer uses this hierarchy to balance speed and cost.
