## History of Computers

### The Five Generations

| Generation | Period | Technology | Example |
|-----------|--------|-----------|---------|
| 1st | 1940-1956 | Vacuum Tubes | ENIAC |
| 2nd | 1956-1963 | Transistors | IBM 1401 |
| 3rd | 1964-1971 | Integrated Circuits | IBM 360 |
| 4th | 1971-Present | Microprocessors | PC, Mac |
| 5th | Present-Future | AI & Quantum | Modern AI systems |

### Key Figures

- **Charles Babbage** — Designed the Analytical Engine (mechanical computer)
- **Ada Lovelace** — First programmer (wrote algorithms for Babbage's machine)
- **Alan Turing** — Father of theoretical computer science, cracked WWII codes
- **John von Neumann** — Designed the stored-program computer architecture

Computing has evolved from room-sized machines to devices that fit in your pocket — billions of times more powerful.
