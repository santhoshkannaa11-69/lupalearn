## 1. Hook

In 1969, a computer sent the first message over what would become the Internet. The message was "LO." The system crashed after the first two letters.

Today, 5 billion people carry Internet-connected computers in their pockets. That's a device billions of times more powerful than the computers that sent humans to the Moon.

How did we get here — and where are we going?

## 2. Why This Matters

Understanding computing's impact helps you see why your skills matter. Every line of code you'll write builds on decades of innovation — and contributes to what comes next.

## 4. Core Concept

### The Acceleration of Computing

```
1940s: ENIAC — 5,000 ops/sec, room-sized
1960s: Transistor — 100,000 ops/sec, cabinet-sized
1980s: PC — 1M ops/sec, desk-sized
2000s: Laptop — 1B ops/sec, bag-sized
2020s: Phone — 10B+ ops/sec, pocket-sized
```

Computing power doubles roughly every 2 years (Moore's Law).

## 5. Sector Transformations

### Communication

| Then | Now |
|------|-----|
| Letters (days) | Email (seconds) |
| Phone calls (wired) | Video calls (mobile) |
| Newspapers (daily) | News feeds (real-time) |

### Healthcare

Computers enable: MRI and CT scanning, robotic surgery, telemedicine, AI-assisted diagnosis, personalized medicine through genomics.

### Finance

Stock trading went from floor shouting to algorithmic trading (millions of orders per second). Banking went from branch visits to mobile apps.

### Science

Computers enabled: human genome sequencing, climate modeling, particle physics (CERN generates 30 petabytes/year), drug discovery through simulation.

## 6. The AI Revolution

Artificial intelligence is the next frontier:

| Era | Capability | Example |
|-----|-----------|---------|
| **2000s** | Pattern recognition | Spam filters, handwriting recognition |
| **2010s** | Understanding | Speech recognition, image classification |
| **2020s** | Generation | Text, image, music, code creation |
| **2030s?** | Autonomy | Self-driving, scientific discovery |
