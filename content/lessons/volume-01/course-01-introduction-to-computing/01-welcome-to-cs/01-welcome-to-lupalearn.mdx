## 1. Hook — Spark Curiosity

Think about the last time you used your phone.

Maybe you checked Instagram, ordered food, or watched a YouTube video.

Now think about this: every single one of those actions — the scroll, the swipe, the recommendation, the payment — was made possible by someone writing code.

And the person who wrote that code? They started exactly where you are right now: on Lesson 1.

## 2. Why This Matters

Computer Science isn't about learning a programming language. It's about learning how to think.

The skills you'll build — breaking problems into steps, recognizing patterns, testing ideas, learning from failure — are valuable in every field:

| Field | How CS Applies |
|-------|---------------|
| **Medicine** | MRI imaging, drug discovery, robotic surgery |
| **Finance** | Algorithmic trading, fraud detection |
| **Music** | Digital production, streaming, composition |
| **Sports** | Performance analytics, strategy optimization |
| **Art** | Digital tools, generative AI, animation |

CS is not just for programmers. It's for anyone who wants to understand how the modern world works.

## 4. Core Concept

### What Is Computer Science?

**Computer Science** is the study of problems, problem-solving, and the systems we build to solve them.

It's not about computers — computers are just the tool. CS is about:

```
Problem → Think → Design → Build → Test → Solve
```

### The School of Computer Science

This is the complete path ahead of you:

## 5. Mental Model — Analogy

Think of learning CS like learning to cook.

| Learning CS | Learning to Cook |
|-------------|------------------|
| Variables | Ingredients |
| Functions | Recipes |
| Loops | Repeating a technique |
| Debugging | Tasting and adjusting |
| Projects | Cooking a full meal |

You don't learn to cook by memorizing every recipe. You learn by understanding techniques, practicing them, and eventually creating your own dishes.

Same with programming.
