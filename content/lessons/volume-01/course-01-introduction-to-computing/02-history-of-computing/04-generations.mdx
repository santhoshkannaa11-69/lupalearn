## 1. Hook

The phone in your pocket is millions of times more powerful than the computer that guided Apollo 11 to the Moon. That's not gradual improvement — that's a revolution that happened five times.

## 2. Why This Matters

Each generation introduced a fundamental breakthrough that made computers smaller, faster, cheaper, and more accessible. Understanding this progression helps you appreciate why computing evolves the way it does.

## 3. The Five Generations

| Gen | Period | Technology | Size | Speed | Cost |
|-----|--------|-----------|------|-------|------|
| 1st | 1940-56 | Vacuum tubes | Room | ~5K ops/s | Millions |
| 2nd | 1956-63 | Transistors | Cabinet | ~100K ops/s | Hundreds of K |
| 3rd | 1964-71 | ICs | Desk | ~1M ops/s | Tens of K |
| 4th | 1971-now | Microprocessor | Laptop | ~1B ops/s | Thousands |
| 5th | Now+ | AI + Parallel | Anywhere | ~10B+ ops/s | Hundreds |

## 4. What Each Generation Enabled

| Generation | Breakthrough | What It Enabled |
|-----------|-------------|-----------------|
| **Vacuum tubes** | First electronic computers | Codebreaking, scientific computing |
| **Transistors** | Reliability + smaller size | Business computing, early languages |
| **Integrated Circuits** | Multiple transistors on one chip | Minicomputers, more accessible |
| **Microprocessors** | Entire CPU on one chip | Personal computers, smartphones |
| **AI & Parallel** | Learning, massive parallelism | Self-driving cars, language models |

## 5. Summary

- Computers evolved through **5 technology generations**
- Each generation made computers **smaller, faster, and cheaper**
- The microprocessor (4th gen) enabled the PC revolution
- We're now in the **AI generation** — machines that learn

## 6. What's Next

Where is computing headed? The final lesson of this module explores **quantum computing, AI, edge computing, and neuromorphic chips** — the technologies that will define the next generation.
