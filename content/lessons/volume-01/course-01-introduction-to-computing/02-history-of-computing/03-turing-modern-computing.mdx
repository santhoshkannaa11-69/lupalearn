## Alan Turing and Modern Computing

**Alan Turing** (1912-1954) made foundational contributions to computer science.

### The Turing Machine (1936)

Turing conceived a simple theoretical device that could compute anything computable. The **Turing Machine** has:

1. An infinitely long **tape** divided into cells
2. A **head** that reads and writes symbols
3. A set of **rules** that determine behavior

This simple model is the foundation of all modern computing.

### The Entscheidungsproblem

Turing proved that some problems cannot be solved by any algorithm — the **halting problem** is undecidable. This established the limits of computation.

### Turing Test (1950)

Turing proposed a test: if a machine can converse with a human without the human realizing it's a machine, it demonstrates intelligence. This remains a benchmark in AI.

### WWII Codebreaking

During World War II, Turing designed machines to break the German Enigma code. His work shortened the war and saved millions of lives.

> "We can only see a short distance ahead, but we can see plenty there that needs to be done."
> — Alan Turing

The "Turing Award" is the highest honor in computer science — the Nobel Prize of computing.
