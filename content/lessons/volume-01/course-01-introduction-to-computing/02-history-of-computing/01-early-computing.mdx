## Early Computing Devices

Before electronic computers, humans used mechanical devices to calculate.

### The Abacus (c. 2500 BCE)

The abacus is the earliest known computing device. Beads on rods represent numbers, and users perform calculations by moving them.

- Still used in some parts of Asia today
- Skilled users can calculate faster than electronic calculators for some operations

### Napier's Bones (1617)

John Napier invented numbered rods that simplified multiplication by converting it to addition.

### The Slide Rule (1620-1970s)

A mechanical analog computer that performed multiplication, division, and logarithms. Used by engineers for over 300 years.

### Pascaline (1642)

Blaise Pascal invented a mechanical calculator that could add and subtract numbers directly. It used gears and wheels — like a car's odometer.

### Difference Engine (1822)

Charles Babbage designed the Difference Engine — a mechanical device that could compute polynomial functions automatically. It was the first concept of a programmable computer.

These devices laid the foundation for the electronic computers that followed.
