## 1. Hook

Today's computers manipulate billions of transistors on a chip. Tomorrow's computers might manipulate individual atoms, light particles, or neural connections — fundamentally changing what "computing" means.

## 2. Emerging Technologies

### Quantum Computing

Uses **qubits** that can be 0, 1, or both simultaneously (superposition).

| Classical | Quantum |
|-----------|---------|
| Bit = 0 or 1 | Qubit = 0, 1, or both |
| Transistors | Superconducting circuits |
| Binary logic | Quantum gates |
| Sequential | Probabilistic |

Potential impact: drug discovery, cryptography, climate modeling, optimization.

### Edge Computing

Processing data on local devices instead of sending everything to the cloud.

- Faster response (milliseconds vs seconds)
- Privacy (data stays on device)
- Works offline

### Neuromorphic Computing

Chips designed to mimic the brain's neural structure. Potentially millions of times more energy-efficient than current processors.

## 3. The Exponential Trend

```
1940: 1 computer in the world
1970: 1 computer per university
1990: 1 computer per company
2010: 1 computer per person
2030: Computing everywhere — embedded, wearable, ambient
```

## 4. Summary

- **Quantum computing** will solve problems impossible for classical computers
- **Edge computing** brings processing closer to the user
- **Neuromorphic chips** mimic the brain for energy efficiency
- Computing is becoming **invisible and ubiquitous**
