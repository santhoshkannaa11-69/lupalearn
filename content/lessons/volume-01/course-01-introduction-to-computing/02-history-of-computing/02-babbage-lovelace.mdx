## Charles Babbage & Ada Lovelace

Two of the most important figures in computing history.

### Charles Babbage (1791-1871)

Known as the **"Father of the Computer"**, Babbage designed:

| Machine | Year | Purpose |
|---------|------|---------|
| Difference Engine | 1822 | Calculate polynomial functions |
| Analytical Engine | 1837 | General-purpose programmable computer |

The Analytical Engine was remarkable because it had:

- An **arithmetic logic unit** (the "mill")
- **Memory** (the "store")
- A **control unit** using punch cards
- The ability to loop and branch

### Ada Lovelace (1815-1852)

Ada Lovelace was a mathematician who worked with Babbage. She is considered the **first computer programmer**.

- She wrote the first algorithm intended for machine execution
- She understood that computers could do more than calculate numbers — they could process any symbolic information
- She envisioned computers creating music and art

> "The Analytical Engine has no pretensions to originate anything. It can do whatever we know how to order it to perform."
> — Ada Lovelace

Ada's vision of computing went far beyond what anyone imagined at the time.
