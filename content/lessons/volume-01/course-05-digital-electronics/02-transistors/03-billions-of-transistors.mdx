## 1. Hook

A modern CPU has over 10 billion transistors. If you lined them up side by side, they'd stretch for thousands of kilometers. Yet they all work together seamlessly, switching billions of times per second.

## 2. The Scale Problem

| CPU | Year | Transistors | If you counted one per second... |
|-----|------|-------------|----------------------------------|
| Intel 4004 | 1971 | 2,300 | ~38 minutes |
| Pentium | 1993 | 3.1 million | ~36 days |
| Core i9 | 2023 | ~10 billion | ~317 years |

## 3. How They Work Together

Transistors are organized hierarchically:

```
Transistors (billions)
    → Logic Gates (millions)
        → Circuit Blocks (thousands)
            → Functional Units (hundreds)
                → CPU Core
```

Each layer abstracts complexity. The designer thinks about CPU cores, not individual transistors.

## 4. Summary

- Modern chips have **billions of transistors**
- They're organized in a **hierarchy** from transistors to cores
- **Abstraction** hides the complexity at each level
