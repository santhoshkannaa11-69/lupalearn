## 1. Hook

You've learned AND as a logical concept: A·B = output 1 when both are 1. Now you'll see AND as a physical circuit — two transistors in series controlling current flow.

## 2. AND Gate Circuit

```
Vcc (+)
  │
  ├── [T1] Gate = A
  │      │
  │      └── [T2] Gate = B
  │             │
  │             └── Output (A AND B)
  │
  └── Pull-down resistor to GND
```

When both A AND B are high (1), both transistors conduct, and the output is connected to Vcc (1).

| A | B | T1 | T2 | Output |
|---|---|----|----|--------|
| 0 | 0 | OFF | OFF | 0 |
| 0 | 1 | OFF | ON | 0 |
| 1 | 0 | ON | OFF | 0 |
| 1 | 1 | ON | ON | 1 |

## 3. Transistor Count

| Gate | Transistors | Why |
|------|-------------|-----|
| AND | 6 | NAND + NOT |
| OR | 6 | NOR + NOT |
| NOT | 2 | Simplest gate |
| NAND | 4 | Most efficient |
| NOR | 4 | Also efficient |

## 4. Summary

- An **AND gate** = two transistors in series
- Different gates need **different numbers of transistors**
- NAND and NOR are the **most efficient** (fewest transistors)
