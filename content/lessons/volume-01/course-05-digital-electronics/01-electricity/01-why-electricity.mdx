## 1. Hook

Your computer runs on electricity — not because electricity is the only way to compute, but because it's the fastest, most reliable way we've found to represent two states: on and off.

## 2. Why Electricity?

| Requirement | How Electricity Solves It |
|-------------|--------------------------|
| **Two clear states** | Voltage present (1) or not (0) |
| **Speed** | Electrons move at near light speed |
| **Reliability** | Solid-state switches last billions of cycles |
| **Miniaturization** | Transistors can be atom-scale |

## 3. Analog vs Digital Signals

**Analog**: Continuous voltage — can be any value. Susceptible to noise.

**Digital**: Discrete voltage levels — 0 or 1. Noise-resistant.

``` 
Analog: ───/\/\/\───/\/\─── (continuous wave)
Digital: ████░░░░████░░░░ (discrete levels)
```

## 4. Summary

- Computers use electricity because **switches** (on/off) map perfectly to **binary** (1/0)
- **Digital signals** are more reliable than analog for computing
- Every component in your computer is ultimately controlled by electricity
