## 1. Hook

A light switch has two positions: up (ON) and down (OFF). A transistor has two states: conducting (ON) and not conducting (OFF). This is why binary is the natural language of computers — it maps directly to physical switches.

## 2. Switch → Binary → Logic

```
Physical switch:  [ON]   [OFF]
                     ↓      ↓
Binary:             1      0
                     ↓      ↓
Boolean:          true   false
                     ↓      ↓
Programming:     true   false
```

## 3. The Magic of Combining Switches

Two switches in **series** = AND:
```
Switch A AND Switch B must both be ON → light turns on
```

Two switches in **parallel** = OR:
```
Switch A OR Switch B must be ON → light turns on
```

## 4. Summary

- **Switches** are the physical foundation of binary computing
- **Series** switches = AND, **Parallel** switches = OR
- Billions of microscopic switches (transistors) inside a CPU create all computation
