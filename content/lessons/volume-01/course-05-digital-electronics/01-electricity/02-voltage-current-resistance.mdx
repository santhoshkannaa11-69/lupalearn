## 1. Hook

Think of electricity like water in a pipe. Voltage is the water pressure. Current is the amount of water flowing. Resistance is the pipe's width. This analogy makes the three most important electrical concepts intuitive.

## 2. The Water Analogy

| Electrical Term | Water Analogy | Unit |
|----------------|---------------|------|
| **Voltage** | Water pressure | Volts (V) |
| **Current** | Water flow rate | Amperes (A) |
| **Resistance** | Pipe narrowness | Ohms (Ω) |

### Ohm's Law (Simplified)

```
Voltage = Current × Resistance
V = I × R

Higher voltage → more current can flow
Higher resistance → less current flows
```

## 3. Why This Matters for Computing

| Component | Uses | Electrical Property |
|-----------|------|---------------------|
| **CPU** | Billions of transistors switching | Current switching at GHz speeds |
| **RAM** | Capacitors storing charge | Voltage held or released |
| **PSU** | Provides stable power | Regulated voltage and current |
| **USB** | Data + power delivery | 5V, up to 3A |

## 4. Summary

- **Voltage** = electrical pressure, **Current** = flow, **Resistance** = opposition
- **Ohm's Law** relates all three (V = I × R)
- Computers manage voltage and current at incredible speeds and tiny scales
