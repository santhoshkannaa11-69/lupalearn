# What Is a Graph?

## One Sentence
A graph is a collection of vertices (nodes) connected by edges — it's a way of modeling relationships between things.

## Why Should Someone Care?
Graphs are everywhere. Social networks (who is friends with whom), maps (shortest route), the internet (web pages linking to each other), and even LupaLearn's knowledge graph. Understanding graphs means understanding how the modern connected world works.

## Teaching Goals
**Know:** Vertex, edge, directed vs undirected, degree
**Do:** Identify vertices and edges, draw a graph, classify directed/undirected
**Stop believing:** Graphs = bar charts, graphs are only for math

## Best Analogy
⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐ **City Map** — Cities are vertices, roads are edges, one-way streets are directed edges

## ASCII Visuals
```
Undirected:   ① — ②
              |    |
              ③ — ④

Directed:     ① → ②
              ↑    ↓
              ③ ← ④
```

## Common Mistakes
- Confusing graphs with bar charts
- Thinking all graphs are trees
- Forgetting edges can have direction
