# Cartesian Products

## One Sentence
The Cartesian product of two sets A and B (written A × B) is the set of all possible ordered pairs (a, b) where a ∈ A and b ∈ B.

## Why Should Someone Care?
Cartesian products are the foundation of:
- Coordinates in graphics (x, y)
- Database tables (rows are combinations of columns)
- Relations in databases
- Functions (input-output pairs)
- Grids and matrices in programming

## Teaching Goals
**Know:** Definition of A × B, ordered pairs, cardinality |A × B| = |A| × |B|
**Do:** Compute the Cartesian product of two small sets
**Stop believing:** That A × B is the same as B × A (ordered pairs make them different)

## Best Analogy
⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐ **Menu Combos**
A restaurant has appetizers {soup, salad} and mains {pasta, steak}. All possible combos: {(soup, pasta), (soup, steak), (salad, pasta), (salad, steak)}. That's the Cartesian product.

## Common Mistakes
- Thinking A × B = B × A
- Forgetting that (a, b) ≠ (b, a)
- Confusing Cartesian product with union

## Quiz Ideas
1. If A={1,2}, B={x,y}, what is A×B?
2. What is |A×B| if |A|=3 and |B|=4?
3. Is (1,2) the same as (2,1)?
4. What if one set is empty?
