# The Contact List Problem

Maria just got a new phone. She exported her contacts from her old phone. Her friend sent her a contacts file too. Now she has 847 contacts — but she knows many are duplicates. Some are stored as "Mom" in one list and "Mom (Home)" in another.

She needs to figure out:

1. Who is unique?
2. Who appears in both lists?
3. How many total unique contacts does she have?

This is a set problem.

Without understanding sets, Maria would manually scan 847 names. With sets, she writes three lines of code and gets her answer instantly.

This is why sets matter — they solve real problems that lists can't.
